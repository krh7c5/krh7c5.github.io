Making Observations

Making observations is a skill that is necessary in this course and will help you when conducting an experiement. There are two types of observations; qualitative and quantitative. Both require the use of one or more of the five senses: Touch, Taste, Smell, Hear, and sight. C.E.J. or "Claim" , "Evidence", and "Justification"

In any class or situation, a CEJ format is an excellent model to follow when making a claim. In science, we will follow this model several times throughout the year. You will be researching information that pertains to your claim to determine if any evidence exists to support your claim. Using and citing this information with validate your claim and help you in writing a justification. As explained in the video, the justification is an opportunity to explain how your claim addresses a scientific question or problem. In addition, key concepts that are involved are explained. In the end, you have a solid argument of how and why your claim addresses an issue or solves a problem.
